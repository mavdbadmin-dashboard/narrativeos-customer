FAQs

Common questions, honest answers.

How long does content removal actually take? +

It depends on the platform and content type. Google delisting requests typically take 2–6 weeks. DMCA takedowns on hosting platforms: 1–2 weeks. Direct platform removals vary widely — some within days, others requiring escalation. Data broker opt-outs: 30–90 days. We track every request and provide status updates. Our overall success rate on actionable content exceeds 90%.

Can you remove content from news websites and Reddit? +

Sometimes directly, always indirectly. Direct removal from news sites requires valid legal grounds (defamation, privacy violations, factual errors) or a willing publisher. Reddit removals require content policy violations. When direct removal isn't possible, we use our SEO suppression system to push the content below page two of Google — effectively making it invisible to 99% of searchers.

What is LLM Hallucination Defense and why does it matter? +

AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews synthesize information from their training data to answer questions. When that training data includes inaccurate, outdated, or defamatory content about you, the AI repeats it as fact — to millions of users daily. Our LLM Defense service monitors AI outputs for false information, then deploys authoritative source content and structured data to correct the record and prevent future hallucinations.

Are there any lock-in contracts? +

No. All monthly plans are month-to-month. You can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel at any time with 30 days notice. We believe our results speak for themselves — we don't need contracts to keep clients. Project-based services (Pre-IPO cleanup, Wikipedia creation) have defined scopes with agreed timelines but no ongoing obligation.

Is this service appropriate for public companies? +

Yes, and we specialize in it. Our team understands SEC disclosure requirements, Reg FD compliance, IR communication norms, and the specific reputation dynamics of publicly traded companies — including short-seller attack campaigns, analyst perception management, and investor-facing narrative alignment. This is one of our key differentiators from generalist ORM agencies.

What's the difference between the Essential and Professional plans? +

The Essential plan covers monitoring, light content production, and basic data broker removal — it's a strong foundation. Professional activates active removal campaigns (targeting 40+ brokers and requesting delisting), scales content production to 30 pieces/month for SEO suppression, manages 4 social platforms, and includes a dedicated Slack channel and monthly strategy call. Most clients with an active reputation problem need Professional or higher.
